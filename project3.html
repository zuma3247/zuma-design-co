Case Study: Al Ansar International School

The Client

Al Ansar International School is an established educational institution with a strong reputation for academic excellence. After years of using the same visual materials, the school administration recognized it was time for a refresh that would better reflect their modern teaching approach and engaged learning environment.

The Challenge

The school's existing materials, including book covers, classroom cards, and educational resources, had become visually dated. While functional, they no longer captured the energy and innovation happening inside their classrooms. The challenge was creating updated designs that would feel fresh and engaging for students while maintaining the professional image parents and administrators expected.

Our Approach

We approached this project by spending time understanding how materials were actually used in classrooms. We interviewed teachers about what worked and what didn't, then developed designs that were both visually appealing and practically useful for everyday teaching. Color coding and clear visual hierarchy made materials easier for students to navigate.

  • Redesigned book covers for multiple subjects and grade levels
  • Created classroom support cards with improved readability
  • Developed a consistent visual system across all educational materials
  • Incorporated the school's colors and identity throughout

The Results

Teachers reported that the new materials made a noticeable difference in their classrooms. Students found the updated designs more engaging, and the improved organization helped with lesson flow. The school administration was pleased with how the refreshed materials aligned with their mission of providing a modern, quality education. The designs have since been adopted across all campuses.
